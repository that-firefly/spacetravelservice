HMRC has issued an advanced ruling for your property expense.

Advanced ruling for Flat A – window replacement

Ruling reference: ARS-2025-00417
Date issued: 23 November 2025

HMRC decision

The cost of replacing the single-glazed windows with double-glazed windows is treated as a revenue expense for tax purposes.

You can claim the full amount of £12,000 as an allowable property expense in your Making Tax Digital submissions.

Reasoning

On the information provided, HMRC considers that:

  • the work restored the property to a good state of repair, rather than substantially improving or altering it
  • double-glazing is now the modern equivalent of the original single-glazed windows
  • the expenditure is therefore classified as a repair, not a capital improvement

This ruling applies only to the specific property and circumstances you described. You should not assume it applies to other properties or different types of work.

What happens next

  • include this expense as an allowable deduction in the relevant MTD period
  • keep this ruling with your records in case HMRC asks about it in the future

You can view this ruling at any time in your Submissions or property details.
